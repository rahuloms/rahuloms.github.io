Food Inflation in India

13 Dec 2020

Consumer Food Price Index

In light of the protest by farmers from Punjab and Haryana over the 3 Farm laws passed in September, I decided to take a look at Food Inflation. The Consumer Price Index (CPI) in India has gone through a few changes since 2010 - the base year has changed from 2010 to 2012. The Consumer Food Price Index is a component of the overall CPI comrising the basket of food and beverages and this component remains the the most important basket with a weight of 45.86%. One of the things I noticed while building the graph is that the change of base year has had a significant impact on the resulting inflation figures by bringing the numbers down where they would be even higher for 2020 then what they are now.

Here is the graph below with 10 items comprising the Consumer Food Price Index.

Seasonal fluctuations of Vegetable prices are clear and a big spike in Pluses and Products in 2016 with Vegetables being the overall highest inflating component of the CFPI. Food prices continue to rise rapidly in India - I will do a comparison with other countries soon. In any case, there is a need for reforms in the agri sector to stabilize prices, support farm incomes, diversify crops and most importantly address the stress of farmers from a multitude of reasons including framenting land holdings and rising input costs.
